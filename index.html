Tour Experience

Join us on a full-day multi-transport and walking adventure to 4 of Mark Wiens's favorite local food joints! Navigate with us through back street alleyways, vibrant markets, rail tracks and full urban terrain to gain a local perspective on food communities in Bangkok.

Expect to eat the Thai way - sharing dishes packed with flavor, and tickling your taste buds with a vast array of curries, stir fries, salads, dips and street food snacks on the menu. But be warned, a food coma is guaranteed!

Itinerary

Stop 1: Central Thai food

Our first stop on the tour is Central Thai cuisine - the dishes you're probably most familiar with i.e Pad Thai, Kaeng Khiao wan (Green curry) & Tom Kha Kai (coconut soup). Here you will learn about traditional Thai ingredients and engage with locals to get more familiar with Thai eating culture.

Stop 2: Southern Thai food

After a short digestive walk, we will take you to a Southern Thai restaurant with gorgeous panaromic views of Bangkok. Inspired by Northern Malaysian cuisine, you will taste food packed with pungent flavours of spices such as turmeric. You will try Khua Kling (Dry Yellow Minced Pork Curry) & Gaeng Som Gai (Sour Yellow Chicken Curry) amongst others.

Stop 3: Thai Chinese food

After that we will hop on a tuktuk to Bangkok's Yaowarat district (Chinatown), believed to be the birthplace of Thai Street Food. Here you will be exposed to a variety of wok-based dishes & cooking techniques from different vendors. On the menu will be an array of delicious noodle style dishes, which won't leave you disappointed!

Stop 4: Isaan food

Our final stop will have you tasting the delicacies from Isaan - the NorthEast. Here you'll have the chance to taste dishes similar to those found in Laos such as Som Dtam (Green Papaya Salad) & Nam Jim, various dipping sauces served on a bed of banana leaves - perfect for sharing!
